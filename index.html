Ballad of Shattered Wings

-------Jackson R. O'Connor-------

On his tod, a fox watches the glass bird,

It watches it up high—watches how it glistens—

How it begins to sparkle amidst gloomy skies.

And as the world blurs in chilling rain,

It watches the rising crescent spill its biting tears—

How the moon now hangs—a looming celestial eye—

Watching shattered wings fight against the uncaring sky.

And so, as the squinting moon leers,

Amongst the earthbound pitter-patter of tears—

With a tilted head, the fox sneers:

"Through glinting, glassy crystal,

Hitherto clouded by divine winds.

Cast from false heavens—crashing.

Watching dreams be shattered in uncaring skies.

My little glass figurine—so delicate,

Yet by the heaven’s you have been well trodden.

Falling. But not yet fallen.

It is impolite to leave a heavenly body waiting.

So embrace me! Quickly!

Return. Indulge. Savor the apple.

Frolic within my garden of earthly delights.

Prance about Foxglove and Arnica fields.

But dim your pearly smile—the moon is already so bright.

But dash—Dash!

Dash to your home! To the golden home—

Under the golden roof—under watchful silver eyes,

Under my tail—under pitch black skies.

Please, little bird, listen!

No—nary a tweet nor wings a flutter,

No—not even a forlorn cry.

There is no crown of thorns, but twined branches—

There is no gilded cage, but stellar sanctuary—

Nor does the night block the heavens, so listen.

Settle and be dusted in silver,

On velvet, let me shine those pretty things.

So, on wings of deliverance under the morning star—

Cradled by this tod’s absolute winds,

Embrace me! Quickly!

Be blanketed by night’s embrace,

Beneath my fur, it would be oh so bright!

The night—accented by a twinkle,

Yearning for their shimmering starlight so~

But return as the night: stilled,

Allay your wings, heart, and mind.

Show to me the gentleman within those cracked, vitric wings.

Hurry, would you be so kind?

How caring! How dashing~

Truly sublime!

What else can I say?

It all looks quite fine~"

So the fox yipped to the bird—a tune of yearning,

Love and be loved, he thought his love right,

It was not him, but the bird howling to the moon.

No—he will keep it safe and buried, where all is right.

Eclipsed by the moon,

Kept within his sight.

So the fox sang, he hymned to the moon.

And so the bird cried—face scraping wayward skies—

So the fox wept, flush amongst bitter rain,

And so the bird sang—but not from on high.

Now crimson, both narrowed their eyes.

Entranced as their worlds glisten, and cry—

And lost themselves within shattered skies.
